What is the Double Helix?

DNA's structure is a double helix — two strands twisted around each other like a spiral staircase. Discovered by Watson and Crick in 1953.

The shape allows DNA to store vast amounts of information compactly and replicate accurately.

The Structure

Sugar-Phosphate Backbone

Outer strands made of alternating sugar (deoxyribose) and phosphate groups. Gives DNA its structure.

Base Pairs

Interior "rungs" connect the two strands. Only 4 bases, but they form all genetic information.

Anti-Parallel

Strands run in opposite directions — one 5' to 3', the other 3' to 5'. Essential for replication.

Major & Minor Groove

The twist creates grooves where proteins can bind to "read" the DNA.

The Four Bases

Pairs always match: A-T and G-C (called complementary base pairing)
