What is Kanban?

A simple system to visualize work and keep it flowing

🍽️

Like a Restaurant Kitchen

Orders come in, they move through prep -> cooking -> plating -> out the door. If the "done" pile backs up, the kitchen slows down and everyone notices. Kanban makes work visible like that.

Kanban (Japanese for "sign" or "card") is a method for managing work that emphasizes continuous delivery without overloading teams. It uses cards on columns to show where work items are at any moment.

📍 Visualize

Every piece of work becomes a card. You can see everything at once — what's pending, what's happening, what's done.

🚦 Limit Work in Progress

Don't start 10 things and finish none. WIP limits force focus. Fewer things half-done = more things done.

➡️ Flow, Don't Push

Pull work when you have capacity, don't get handed more when you're already busy. Keep the line moving.

📊 Measure & Improve

Track how fast work flows. Find bottlenecks. Fix them. Repeat. The board tells you where the jam is.

Visibility

❌ Without Kanban

  • Work hidden in Slack, email, people's heads
  • Nobody knows what's really happening
  • Everything is "high priority"
  • Tasks stuck for weeks, nobody notices
  • Deadlines missed, reasons unclear
  • Team burned out from context switching

✓ With Kanban

  • Everything visible on one screen
  • Real-time status anyone can check
  • WIP limits force trade-offs
  • Bottlenecks stick out like sore thumbs
  • Predictable flow, realistic estimates
  • Team focused, finishing not starting
🚗

It's Like Traffic Management

If one lane is blocked, cars pile up. Kanban is the traffic camera that shows you where the jam is — so you can clear it or redirect flow.
